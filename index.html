Nature Safari Experience in Depth

Make your journey unforgettable. Sri Lanka is the only place on earth where you can see the world’s largest land mammal (Elephant) and the largest marine mammal (Blue Whale) in a single day.

The "Big Five" of Sri Lanka

  • Leopard: Best seen in Yala and Wilpattu.
  • Elephant: Found in almost all parks, but legendary in Minneriya and Udawalawe.
  • Sloth Bear: Most elusive; Wilpattu is the best spot.
  • Blue Whale: Mirissa (South) or Trincomalee (East).
  • Sperm Whale: Mirissa.

📍 Top Safari Destinations

  • Yala National Park: Leopards - Highest leopard density in the world. Stunning coastal scenery.
  • Udawalawe: Wild Elephants - Guaranteed sightings year-round. Feels like an African savanna.
  • Minneriya: "The Gathering" - Up to 300+ elephants gather around the tank (July - Sept).
  • Wilpattu: Lakes & Seclusion - Sri Lanka’s largest park. Famous for natural lakes (villus).
  • Bundala: Birdwatching - UNESCO Biosphere Reserve; home to flamingos.

🛶 Unique Experiences

  • Boat Safaris (Gal Oya): The only place in Sri Lanka where you can watch elephants swimming between islands.
  • Night Trails (Sigiriya / Polonnaruwa): Tracking the elusive Grey Slender Loris with infrared torches.
  • Rainforest Trekking (Sinharaja): A UNESCO World Heritage site for those who prefer walking safaris to explore endemic birds and rare amphibians.

💡 Practical Tips for Your Safari

Best Time to Visit

February to June is generally the best for Yala/Wilpattu (dry season makes animals gather at water holes).

The "Nuwara Eliya" Link

Since you are in the hills, remember that Horton Plains (just 1 hour from The Grand) is perfect for a "Walking Safari" to see Sambar Deer and the endemic Purple-faced Langur.

Responsible Tourism

We kindly ask all guests to stay in jeeps and refrain from feeding animals to preserve the natural habitat for eco-conscious travelers.

Highlands Adventure In Depth

Immerse yourself in the breathtaking beauty of Sri Lanka's hill country. Enjoy scenic rail journeys, cascading waterfalls, vast tea estates, and panoramic mountain hikes.

The Iconic "Blue Train" Journey

Considered one of the most beautiful train rides in the world. Travel from Kandy to Ella winding through misty mountains, lush pine forests, and sprawling tea estates.

Horton Plains & World's End

A stunning plateau offering excellent hikes. Reach "World's End", a sheer cliff with a drop of about 4,000 feet, offering spectacular views on a clear day.

Tea Plantation Immersions

Walk among the lush green rows of world-famous Ceylon tea. Visit historic tea factories to see the process and sample some of the finest brews.

Summit Hikes

Conquer peaks like Ella Rock or Little Adam's Peak for incredible panoramic views. Perfect for early morning sunrises over the hills.

Waterfalls of the Highlands

Sri Lanka’s hill country is dotted with hundreds of spectacular waterfalls. From the famous Lover's Leap in Nuwara Eliya to majestic cascades like Bambarakanda and Ravana, witness the dramatic beauty of these natural falls among the tea estates.
